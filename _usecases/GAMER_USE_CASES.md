# Gamification for Gaming: Use Cases for Gamers

Gamers natively understand the concepts of RNG (random number generation), quests, and achievements. By utilizing distinct **Wheel Types**, the platform can provide them with customized challenges, community trivia, and fresh ways to experience games they've already beaten.

---

## 1. The Default Wheel (RNG & Self-Imposed Rules)
**Concept:** The standard randomizer, heavily used by streamers and casual players alike to introduce chaos into their matches.

### User Scenarios:
*   **"Where Are We Dropping?":** Wheels used by *Apex Legends*, *Fortnite*, or *Call of Duty: Warzone* squads to randomly, and fairly, select their landing zone on the map.
*   **Class/Hero Selectors:** Indecisive players use wheels to pick their Agent in *Valorant*, their tank in *Overwatch*, or their starting class in *Elden Ring*.
*   **Streamer "Handicaps" (Nuzlocke tools):** A Twitch streamer creates a wheel of punishments. Every time they die in game, they spin the wheel (e.g., "Drop your primary weapon," "Invert mouse controls for 5 minutes").

---

## 2. The Quiz Wheel (Esports & Lore Trivia)
**Concept:** Real-time trivia testing deep gaming knowledge.

### User Scenarios:
*   **Item & Stat Tests:** A *League of Legends* or *Dota 2* wheel. Landing on an item prompts the user to identify its exact cost or its specific passive effect from a multiple-choice list.
*   **Retro Gaming Trivia:** A wheel of retro consoles (SNES, Genesis, N64). A spin triggers a question about release dates, flagship titles, or obscure hardware limitations.
*   **Lore Checks:** A *Dark Souls* / *Bloodborne* wheel where landing on a boss triggers a deep-lore question about their backstory.

---

## 3. The Challenge Wheel (The Ultimate Backlog Slayer)
**Concept:** Spins that assign a multi-hour gaming task. The user completes the task and later verifies it via an AI-generated quiz to earn a prestigious badge.

### User Scenarios:
*   **The Steam Backlog Destroyer:** Gamers link or input the 50 unplayed games sitting in their Steam library into a Challenge Wheel. They spin and get *Hollow Knight*. It goes into "Pending." Once they beat it 40 hours later, they take the verification quiz to prove completion, earning the "Backlog Slayer" badge.
*   **Genre Exploration:** A "Try a JRPG" or "Try a Metroidvania" challenge wheel. It assigns a highly-rated game outside of the player's normal comfort zone.
*   **Achievement Hunting:** A wheel of difficult, specific in-game tasks (e.g., "Beat the first boss using only a starting weapon"). Players spin to receive their quest for the evening.

---

## 4. The Content Wheel (Builds & Strategy Guides)
**Concept:** A wheel that acts as an interactive strategy repository.

### User Scenarios:
*   **Random Loadout Generators:** A *Cyberpunk 2077* or *Destiny 2* wheel where spinning lands on a specific build. The pop-up card provides the exact skill tree, weapon combination, and mod list to make the build work.
*   **Leveling Guides:** A wheel for an MMO like *World of Warcraft*. Depending on what class is spun, the card reveals the optimal stats to prioritize while leveling in the new expansion.
